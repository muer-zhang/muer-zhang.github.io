Emotional Intelligence

EQ IQ

1. What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ, short for emotional quotient (also known as emotional intelligence).

2. How is it different to IQ?

EQ is different to IQ. They are both about intelligence but at different perspective. A good example would be at schools, there are always some students who are so smart and it seems like they naturally have the ability to acquire and apply any knowledge. Sometime, we consider them talented.

IQ usually refers to intellectual ability. It includes the ability to solve problem logically, to plan and devise strategies, to understand somethings that's abstract, and to have a good grasp of language. However, EQ is the ability to sense emotions, not only yours, but others' as well. We are then able to use the awareness of emotions to guide our behaviour, i.e. to act conscientiously and to respond to their emotional needs.

3. Why is it important?

EQ plays an important part in this complex world. It is closely related to many aspects in our life We are able to identify our emotions and to gain self awareness. Our surroundings and situations can be different at times, EQ helps us to get adapt to the changes quicker and better. We can empathize with others and communicate with others effectively. EQ also takes part in solving conflicts.

The insight gained from emotionally intelligent tendencies often promotes personal growth and happiness. It is like a superpower and we can cultivate these qualities through attention training, conscious listening and journaling.
