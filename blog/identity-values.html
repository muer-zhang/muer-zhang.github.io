Sprint ONE

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make.

With my previous work, we needed to work as a team together. Teamwork is important. However, there were scenarios that some team members may pick and choose what they prefer to do instead of checking the workflow and the team. This is not fair and inefficient for the whole team. In order to solve this, I suggested to have a catch up and to allocate work to each team members at the beginning of the day. If everyone agrees, itâ€™s great. If not, we solve it right away. This really helps making the day flow smoothly.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I grew up in a family surrounded by my lovely family and friends. Everyone in the family takes care of each other, trust and respect others. My dad is a hard working person. And he spends time with us whenever he can. My mum looks after us and she is always there when we need her. We have a positive attitude towards life and challenges because there is always support behind us.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development.

Strengths: I am cheerful and positive with my learning in this new career path. I am hard working and can work wisely by knowing how to prioritise. I may feel stuck sometimes during my learning but I know when to ask for help or how to get myself to reflect and relax again.

Limitations: This course requires us to get actively involved in discussions and activities via the Slack app. I might be a bit reserved when it comes to share thoughts and talking about myself. But I would like to meet and know these lovely like minded people. I am willing to step out and I did my first introduction on Slack. It felt great.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

During this learning journey, there will be times that learning obstacles appear and I feel stuck. My positive attitude towards challenges will help me get through this. I will do constant reflection and will seek help if needed.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

Let me share a recent example from work. I was assigned to train a new staff at work. There were a lot to learn and remember, hence it was a bit overwhelmed at the beginning. I understood that because I went through that stage as well.

In order to work productively we need to know how to prioritize work flow. We sometimes would come across specimens that require trouble shooting before we could process them to the next stage. One day we came across a specimen that was unlabelled. My colleague was very interested to address the problem straight away. But I suggested that she could carry on with other specimens and leave that problematic specimen for later due to the current work flow.

It was an effective decision. After an hour or so, we finished processing the rest of the specimens and we had half an hour gap to do a catch up. I used my time to explain the whole trouble shooting procedure and she gave it a go. At the same time, we were not delaying other work.

We did a reflection at the end of the day. My colleague learned something new and also understood more about prioritizing work flow. I was also doing reflections on my own and I would like to adjust my training focus to look at the big picture in the team environment. As my new colleague learn more, the focus for her would be able to constantly check her surroundings and to start making decisions of what to prioritize. This is a very important and efficient skill to work together in a team.
